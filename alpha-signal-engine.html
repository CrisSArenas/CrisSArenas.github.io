Understanding This Engine

What This Tool Does

This engine simulates how quantitative researchers discover alpha signals using machine learning. It generates synthetic but realistic factor data, applies ML models to rank signal importance, and shows how factor signals decay over time, correlate with each other, and translate into portfolio returns.

Key Metrics Explained

Gross Sharpe
Risk-adjusted return of the strategy before transaction costs. A Sharpe above 0.5 is considered good; above 1.0 is excellent. Computed as annualized mean return divided by annualized volatility.
Net Sharpe
Same as Gross Sharpe but after deducting estimated transaction costs from turnover.
Ann. Alpha
The annualized excess return generated by the factor signals beyond what the market benchmark delivers. Typical multi-factor alpha is 1-4% per year.
Mean IC
Information Coefficient — measures how well the composite signal predicts future returns. Values of 0.02-0.05 are typical; above 0.05 is strong.
IC IR
IC Information Ratio — the consistency of the IC over time (mean IC / std of IC). Higher means the signal is more reliably predictive each month.
Turnover
Monthly one-way portfolio turnover. Higher turnover increases transaction costs. Momentum strategies turn over ~40-50%; value strategies ~10-15%.
Max DD
Maximum Drawdown — the largest peak-to-trough decline in the strategy equity curve. A key risk measure.
Hit Rate
Percentage of months where the strategy generated positive alpha. Even good strategies typically have hit rates of only 52-58%.

Understanding the Charts

Feature Importance
Shows how much weight the ML model assigns to each factor. Random Forest distributes evenly; Lasso zeroes out weak signals; Gradient Boosting concentrates on the strongest.
Mean IC by Factor
Bar chart showing each factor's average predictive power. Error bars show the range of IC across months. The t-statistic above each bar indicates statistical significance (t > 2.0 means reliably different from zero).
IC Decay Curve
Shows how each factor's predictive power fades over time. Fast-decaying signals (Momentum, Sentiment) lose power within days; slow-decaying signals (Value, Quality) persist for months.
Correlation Matrix
Shows pairwise correlations between factor signals. Low correlation means factors provide independent information. Value-Momentum is typically negative (~-0.30); Momentum-Sentiment is typically positive (~+0.35).
Cumulative Returns
Equity curves for the strategy (gross and net of costs) versus the market benchmark. The spread between strategy and benchmark lines represents cumulative alpha.
Rolling IC
12-month rolling average of the composite signal's Information Coefficient. Shows how signal strength varies through different market regimes.
Turnover Sensitivity
Shows how the strategy's net Sharpe ratio degrades as transaction costs increase. Helps identify the break-even cost level.

Controls Guide

Factor Universe
Toggle individual factors on/off to see how signal composition affects performance. Using more uncorrelated factors generally improves risk-adjusted returns.
ML Model
Switch between models to compare how they select and weight factors. Lasso is best for sparse signal selection; Gradient Boosting for maximizing IC.
Lookback Window
Number of months of historical data used to estimate factor signals. Longer lookbacks provide more stable estimates but may include stale data.
Rebalance Frequency
How often the portfolio is rebalanced. Monthly captures signals fastest but has highest turnover. Quarterly reduces costs but misses short-lived signals.
Transaction Cost
Assumed one-way trading cost in basis points (1bp = 0.01%). Typical institutional costs are 5-20bp depending on market cap and liquidity.

How to Use This Engine

STEP 1

Select Factors

Choose from 8 factor families: Value, Momentum, Quality, Size, Low Volatility, Growth, Sentiment, and Macro Sensitivity.

STEP 2

Choose ML Model

Select a machine learning model: Random Forest, Lasso (L1), Ridge (L2), or Gradient Boosting. Results update live.

STEP 3

Configure Parameters

Set lookback window, rebalance frequency, and transaction costs. All charts update instantly.

STEP 4

Explore Charts

Hover over any chart to see detailed values. Crosshairs and tooltips reveal data at every point.
