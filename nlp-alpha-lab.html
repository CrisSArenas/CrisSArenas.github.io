Understanding This Lab

What This Tool Does

This lab demonstrates how NLP (Natural Language Processing) extracts investment signals from earnings call transcripts. It scores individual words for sentiment, aggregates them into document-level signals, and combines them with alternative data sources to generate alpha predictions that are backtested against a market benchmark.

Reading the Attention Heatmap

Each word in the transcript is scored by the NLP model. Green highlighted words are positive sentiment (e.g., "record-breaking", "exceeded", "outstanding"). Red highlighted words are negative sentiment (e.g., "declined", "deteriorated", "compressed"). Intensity indicates confidence: darker shading means a stronger signal.

The model focuses on financially relevant adjectives and verbs, not neutral nouns or connectors. This mimics how transformer-based models like FinBERT assign attention weights.

Key Metrics Explained

Sentiment
The overall document sentiment score from -1 (very negative) to +1 (very positive). Aggregated from word-level scores across the full transcript. AAPL and NVDA tend positive; TSLA tends negative.
Signal Str.
Combined signal strength from all active alternative data sources. Represents the total alpha potential of the selected signal combination.
NLP Sharpe
Sharpe ratio of the NLP composite strategy. Measures risk-adjusted total returns. Values of 0.7-1.3 are typical for well-constructed NLP alpha strategies.
Signal IC
Information Coefficient at the selected holding period. Measures how well the composite signal predicts forward returns. NLP signals typically have IC of 0.02-0.04.
Ann. Alpha
Annualized alpha (excess return over benchmark) generated by the composite NLP strategy. Realistic NLP alpha is 1-4% annually.
Half-Life
Number of trading days until the composite signal loses half its predictive power. NLP signals typically have half-lives of 20-60 days, shorter than fundamental signals.

Understanding the Charts

Sentence Sentiment
Bar chart decomposing the transcript into individual sentences. Bars above zero are positive sentences; below zero are negative. Shows how sentiment is distributed through the earnings call (CEO vs CFO vs Q&A).
Sector Heatmap
Cross-sector sentiment derived from the selected transcript. Shows how the NLP model extrapolates company-specific sentiment to broader sectors based on supply chain and industry linkages.
Signal Decay Curve
Shows how each signal's IC (predictive power) fades over forward holding days 1-63. Options Flow decays fastest (short-lived); Supply Chain and Analyst Revisions persist longest.
Sentiment vs Return
Scatter plot of NLP sentiment score vs forward period returns across a simulated cross-section of stocks. The regression line slope and IC annotation show the signal's predictive relationship.
Cumulative Alpha Returns
Backtest equity curves over 60 months for: NLP Composite (all signals), NLP Only (sentiment signal alone), Traditional Factors (without NLP), and Benchmark. Red shading marks drawdown periods.
Signal Contribution
Horizontal bars showing each signal's weight in the composite, along with its IC and alpha contribution. Helps understand which alternative data source drives the most value.

Alternative Data Signals

NLP Sentiment
Derived from earnings call transcripts using word-level sentiment scoring. Captures management tone and language that may predict future stock performance.
Analyst Revisions
Tracks changes in sell-side analyst earnings estimates. Upward revisions signal improving fundamentals. Typically the most persistent alternative signal (highest IC).
Options Flow
Monitors unusual options activity (put/call ratios, volume spikes). Can indicate informed trading. Fast-decaying signal with lower IC but provides independent information.
Supply Chain
Analyzes supply chain data (shipping volumes, supplier sentiment, inventory levels). Slower-moving signal that captures fundamental trends before they appear in financial statements.

Controls Guide

Earnings Transcript
Select from 4 company transcripts with different sentiment profiles. AAPL and NVDA are positive; META is mixed; TSLA is negative. Each changes all charts and metrics.
Signal Toggles
Enable/disable individual alternative data signals. Combining uncorrelated signals generally improves risk-adjusted returns. At least one signal must remain active.
Holding Period
Forward holding period in trading days (5-63). Shorter periods capture faster signals but have higher turnover. Longer periods let slow signals compound but fast signals decay.

Performance Table

Ann. Return is total annualized return including benchmark. Ann. Alpha is the excess return over benchmark. Sharpe (alpha) is the information ratio of the alpha stream. Max Drawdown shows worst peak-to-trough decline. Signal IC is the composite signal's Information Coefficient at the selected holding period.

How This Lab Works

STEP 1
